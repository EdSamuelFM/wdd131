Filament Comparison: Choosing Your Material

The filament you choose dictates the final properties of your print, including its strength, temperature resistance, and flexibility. Most beginners start with PLA due to its ease of use, but specialized materials like PETG and ABS offer much higher performance for functional parts.

Material Notes:

PLA (Polylactic Acid): Best for visual models and prototyping. Requires the lowest temperatures (minimal warping) and is derived from renewable resources, making it low-fume. Always use a cooling fan to ensure crisp details.

PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol): A popular bridge between PLA and ABS. Offers high strength, good temperature resistance, and minimal odor. It tends to be sticky and requires careful Z-height calibration to prevent print blobs (stringing).

ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene): Very strong, durable, and highly temperature resistant. Requires an enclosure and higher temperatures to prevent warping and cracking. Always print in a well-ventilated area due to strong fumes.

TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane): Known for its flexibility. Print speeds must be significantly reduced (15-30 mm/s) due to the soft nature of the material, and a direct drive extruder is highly recommended over bowden setups.
