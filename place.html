About Porto Alegre

Porto Alegre is the capital city of Rio Grande do Sul, the southernmost state in Brazil. With a population of over 1.4 million people, it's known for its high quality of life, cultural richness, and being a major educational and political center.

The city is located at the junction of five rivers, forming the Guaíba River, which widens into a vast waterway. Porto Alegre has a rich cultural scene with numerous theaters, museums, and galleries, and is famous for its traditional gaúcho culture.

Top Attractions

Public Market

The historic Mercado Público is a cultural landmark offering local foods, crafts, and traditional restaurants.

Guaíba Riverfront

The Orla do Guaíba offers stunning sunset views and recreational spaces along the city's waterfront.

Farroupilha Park

Also known as Redenção, this is the city's most popular park with trails, lakes, and cultural events.
